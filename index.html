Project Mercury(1961–1963)

Project Mercury was a groundbreaking endeavor in the history of space exploration, serving as the United States' initial human spaceflight program.

Giotto mission(1985)

The Giotto mission was a landmark achievement in cometary exploration, launched by the European Space Agency (ESA) in 1985.

Mir Space Station (1986–2001)

The Mir space station, operating from 1986 to 2001, was a significant achievement in space exploration, initially for the Soviet Union and later for the Russian Federation. Its name, "Mir," meaning "peace" or "world" in Russian, reflected its purpose as a research outpost and later as a platform for international cooperation.

Chandrayaan-1 (2008–2009)

Chandrayaan is a series of Indian lunar missions developed by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO) to explore the Moon. Chandrayaan-1, launched in 2008, was India's first mission to the Moon and made the significant discovery of water molecules on the lunar surface

Apollo 11 (1969)

Apollo 11 was a pivotal mission in the history of space exploration, marking the first time humans set foot on the surface of another celestial body. It was launched by NASA as part of the Apollo program, a national effort to fulfill President John F. Kennedy's goal of landing a man on the Moon and returning him safely to Earth before the end of the 1960s.

Mars Express (2003–present)

The Mars Express mission, launched by the European Space Agency (ESA) in 2003, is a long-lived and highly successful planetary exploration effort aimed at studying Mars. The mission is comprised of an orbiter and a lander, Beagle 2, although Beagle 2 experienced operational failure after landing on the Martian surface.

Sputnik 1 (1957)

Sputnik 1, launched by the Soviet Union on October 4, 1957, was the world's first artificial satellite.

Aditya-L1 (2023–present)

The Aditya-L1 mission is India's first space-based solar mission and an observatory-class effort to study the Sun.

Rosetta (2004–2016)

The Rosetta mission was a remarkable endeavor by the European Space Agency (ESA), lasting from 2004 to 2016, with the primary objective of studying a comet: 67P/Churyumov–Gerasimenko. The mission involved both an orbiter and a lander, named Philae.

Vostok 1 (1961)

Vostok 1, launched by the Soviet Union on April 12, 1961, was a monumental achievement in human spaceflight, marking the first time a human being journeyed into outer space and achieved Earth orbit.

Space Shuttle Program (1981–2011)

The Space Shuttle program, officially known as the Space Transportation System (STS), was NASA's fourth human spaceflight program, operating from 1981 to 2011. Its primary goal was to provide routine transportation of crew and cargo to low Earth orbit (LEO), which it achieved through its fleet of reusable spacecraft.

ASTROSAT (2015–2022):

ASTROSAT is India's first dedicated multi-wavelength space observatory, launched by the Indian Space Research Organisation (ISRO). It was operational from 2015 to 2022, significantly contributing to various areas of astronomical research.

Frequently Asked Questions

Have more questions? Feel free to reach out to our support team.

Who was the first human in space?

Yuri Gagarin, a Soviet cosmonaut, became the first human in space on April 12, 1961, orbiting Earth in Vostok 1.

What was the first artificial satellite?

Sputnik 1, launched by the Soviet Union on October 4, 1957, was the first artificial satellite to orbit Earth.

How does a rocket blast off?

Rockets burn fuel to produce hot, expanding gas that is expelled through a nozzle, generating thrust to lift off from Earth.

What do astronauts wear in space?

Astronauts wear specially designed spacesuits that provide life support, protection from radiation, and mobility in the harsh space environment.

What is a space probe?

A space probe is an uncrewed spacecraft sent to explore outer space, gathering data about planets, moons, and other celestial bodies.
